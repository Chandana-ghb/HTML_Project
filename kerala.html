Backwaters & Munnar

The Kerala backwaters are a unique network of interconnected canals, rivers, lakes, and lagoons that stretch parallel to the Arabian Sea coast. This serene water world is best experienced through a traditional houseboat cruise, known locally as a kettuvallam. As you drift through the backwaters, you'll witness lush paddy fields, swaying coconut palms, and glimpses of daily village life.

Kerala, often referred to as "God's Own Country", is a stunning state located on the southwestern coast of India, renowned for its lush green landscapes, serene backwaters, and rich cultural heritage. Blessed with a unique geography that includes tranquil beaches, misty hill stations, and sprawling tea plantations, Kerala offers a diverse and immersive travel experience. The state is also famous for its traditional art forms like Kathakali and Mohiniyattam, vibrant festivals such as Onam, and Ayurvedic therapies that attract visitors from around the globe.

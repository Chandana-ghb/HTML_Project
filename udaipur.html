Udaipur

Udaipur, often called the "City of Lakes", is a beautiful city in the southern part of Rajasthan, India. Founded in 1559 by Maharana Udai Singh II, it was the capital of the Mewar Kingdom. Udaipur is famous for its romantic charm, royal heritage, and stunning lakes, such as Lake Pichola, Fateh Sagar Lake, and Udai Sagar Lake.

Rajasthan is the largest state in India by area, located in the northwestern part of the country. Known as the "Land of Kings", it is famous for its rich history, royal heritage, grand forts, palaces, and vibrant culture. The capital city is Jaipur, also called the Pink City, and other major cities include Udaipur, Jodhpur, Bikaner, Ajmer, and Jaisalmer. Rajasthan is home to majestic forts like Mehrangarh (Jodhpur), Amber Fort (Jaipur), Chittorgarh, and Jaisalmer Fort. The state showcases a unique desert landscape, especially the Thar Desert, and experiences extreme temperatures. Despite the arid climate, Rajasthan has a colorful and lively culture, known for its folk music, dances, art, festivals, and traditional attire. With its blend of ancient history, desert beauty, and cultural richness, Rajasthan is one of the most iconic and visited states in India.

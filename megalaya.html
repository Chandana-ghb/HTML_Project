Shillong & Cherrapunji

Shillong is the capital city of Meghalaya, often called the "Scotland of the East" due to its rolling hills, pleasant climate, and colonial-era architecture. It is located at an altitude of about 1,496 meters (4,908 ft) above sea level. Known for its pine-covered hills, waterfalls, and cultural charm, Shillong is a popular hill station in northeast India. The city is a cultural hub for the Khasi tribe and showcases a blend of modern and traditional lifestyles.

Meghalaya is a beautiful state in northeastern India, known for its lush green landscapes, heavy rainfall, and unique culture. The name "Meghalaya" means "Abode of Clouds" in Sanskrit. The state was formed in 1972 and its capital is Shillong, often called the "Scotland of the East" because of its scenic beauty and colonial-era charm. Meghalaya is famous for its rolling hills, dense forests, waterfalls, and living root bridges, especially in areas like Cherrapunji and Mawsynram, which are among the wettest places on Earth. The state is home to several indigenous tribes, mainly the Khasi, Garo, and Jaintia, each with their own language, customs, and matrilineal society.
